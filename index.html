03

What is a Short Strategy?

Shorting is a way to profit when a stock goes down. You borrow shares, sell them at today's price, and buy them back later at a lower price — pocketing the difference. The key is timing — the earlier you enter, the more of that decline you capture. Our model gives Riverdale the earliest possible entry, before the market has any indication a recall is coming.

Why the Market Misses NHTSA Pre-Event Signals

  • NHTSA data is public but requires a multi-source pipeline to extract signal from noise
  • Complaint velocity, TSB component matching, and investigation timing must be computed together across 1.98M complaints
  • A 2020 peer-reviewed study confirms the market consistently fails to price in NHTSA data before recall announcements — even among sophisticated institutional investors

"The market generally fails to impound the information contained in customer complaints in a timely manner — even among sophisticated institutional investors like mutual funds."

Stage 2 — Position Sizing

Kelly Criterion — Optimal Capital Allocation

A mathematical formula developed by John Kelly at Bell Labs (1956), applied to financial markets by Edward Thorp. Widely used by professional traders and hedge funds to determine the optimal position size given a known win rate and payoff. Not to be confused with Kelly Blue Book.

Full Kelly Formula
f* = (p × b − q) / b
f* = optimal fraction | p = win probability | b = win size / stop loss | q = 1 − p
In Simple Terms
Position = (how often it works × how much you make) minus (how often it fails × your stop loss)
Position Scaling Formula
Final Position = Base × (Signal Probability / Threshold)
Capped at $1,000,000 (10% of AUM) | Floored at $50,000 (0.5% of AUM)
p — Win Rate
From Recall Impact Scorecard — how often this company's stock drops more than 2% on recall days
Base rate: 21.9% across all 3,631 recall events
b — Win Size
Average drop size divided by stop loss. Bigger drops = better risk/reward ratio
Example — Lucid: -11.66% avg drop → positive Kelly → $604,000 base position
q — Loss Rate (1−p)
How often the stock does NOT drop and the short loses to the stop loss
Stop loss fixed at 5% — maximum loss per trade before exit
Half-Kelly Applied
We use half the Kelly fraction (f*/2) to account for estimation error and reduce volatility
Standard institutional practice for Kelly Criterion deployment

Portfolio Parameters

  • Portfolio size: $10,000,000 — consistent with emerging manager AUM per The Hedge Fund Journal (2023)
  • Max position: $1,000,000 (10% single-name concentration cap)
  • Min floor: $50,000 (0.5%) — for companies where drops are real but Kelly is negative
  • Top 10 signals per month: Kelly already scales by probability — beyond top 10, signals receive near-floor positions anyway

Manufacturer Risk Intelligence

The same model that identifies short opportunities can be reframed as a proactive risk tool for automakers. A manufacturer could license this system to monitor their own complaint velocity in real time — receiving early warning when a recall is likely, enabling faster voluntary action and protecting stock price before the market reacts.

Manufacturers could use the Recall Impact Scorecard to understand how their stock reacts versus competitors on recall days — informing investor relations strategy and proactive communication when complaint spikes are detected early.
